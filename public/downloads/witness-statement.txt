METROPOLITAN POLICE — WITNESS STATEMENT
=======================================

WITNESS: Arthur Penhallow, night doorman, The Selwyn Hotel
TAKEN BY: Det. Sgt. R. Cole
DATE: November 2, 1948

"I was on the door from nine 'til close. Miss Lockhart came in around
half past ten, alone, in a green coat. Maybe twenty minutes later a
gentleman in a dark overcoat asked for her room by name — 507. I rang
up but there was no answer, so I let him wait in the lobby.

He smelled of that expensive amber cologne, the kind the swells wear.
Left-handed too — signed the guest book with his left. Never caught the
name; the ink was smudged. He went up the service stairs, not the lift.

I didn't see him leave. Around eleven I heard what I thought was a
window slamming on the fifth floor."

Signed: A. Penhallow
